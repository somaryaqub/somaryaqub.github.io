Mission Question:
​ How can we
​ support the whole person
​ flourish in our community?


The whole person includes their mental, emotional, physical, familial, social, economic & spiritual well being.

Flourishing is in accordance with the person's expectations.

Our community includes the many geographical, cultural & faith networks that contribute to our identity.

We believe in a
​ Merciful & Generous Creator.

We begin with gratitude; gratitude for the work we do, who we do it with, and that people have trusted us with what’s difficult. ¶ We seek to imbue our work with mercy and generosity.¶ We work with an abundance of spirit – knowing that we do not have all the answers, that we must be frugal, responsible and transparent, that resources might be scarce, but that our imperative is to do what’s right and what’s right will follow.


We are Created to Serve.

IFSSA is both a means for people to find help and to live our fundamental purpose — to serve. We are being helped when we help. ¶ Serving others is spoken of within the Quran as medicine, and it speaks to the need within; an inner void that is filled through helping others.

“Created to Serve” is derived from Allah’s verse (Surah 3, Ayah 110), “You are the best people ever raised for the good of mankind because you have been raised to serve others.”


We are here to
​ grow (as) people.

We want to be the place that people do their best work. We see our work not as a series of interactions/transactions with clients but an investment in people, team & ourselves. In order to this work we must be developing ourselves.


We are emissaries
​ of a vast tradition.

We are the emissaries of a vast and rich tradition that informs and improves the way we care for people. For more than 30 years IFSSA has been supporting the community through complex challenges by acting on our values.


We are an integral part of the community we serve.

Community is vital to the work we do and who we are. ¶ We seek input, we serve as a platform for good initiatives, we are a starting point and a destination for those who serve. ¶ Because we want to go far we go together.





Who we serve…





Our Community…

IFSSA’s services are open to all, we serve all who come regardless of background while recognizing and addressing the specialized needs of the Muslim community.


Muslims are 5% of Alberta – 190,000 people. Muslims have been a part of Alberta since before Alberta was a province. Muslims are one of the youngest and fastest growing populations in Alberta. [Source: Statistic Canada 2016, analysis by ALIF Partners].


Our Clients…


IFSSA serves 4500 active clients, 1800 families every month — 1 out of 20 Muslims in Edmonton. 70% of IFSSA’s clients don’t use other services like Alberta Works or EMCN [Source: Edmonton’s Food Bank/ Edmonton Social Planning Council 2018 Study]. These are alarming numbers that necessitate us to try different approaches & better understand the needs of our clients.

Security, Safety & Growth

Currently, IFSSA works on addressing the security, safety and growth needs of people through a range of services including culturally appropriate food hampers, crisis support, domestic violence intervention, refugee support, preventative youth programming and by continuing to examine and address systemic challenges.

IFSSA’s clients face barriers when accessing services, these include…

  • Institutional distrust which has complex factors
  • Lack of awareness within systems of cultural conventions, norms, restrictions and preferences
  • Inability to access services due to language, stigma and unfamiliarity
  • Desire for a holistic care perspective that incorporates, mental, emotional, physical, social, & spiritual wellbeing


Concerns

Informing our aims are four primary concerns that put pressure on our team and resources.

  1. 1. Increasing demand
    ​ We now serve more people in one month than we did in all of 2011.
    ​
    ​ We will deal with increasing demand by…
    1. a. Intelligently scaling & focussing on the areas where we have unique strengths and capacities
    2. b. Partnering
    3. c. Better addressing root causes not just symptoms.
      ​
  2. 2. Tougher cases
    ​ The problems we face are increasingly complex and compounded.
    ​ We will address tougher cases through…
    1. a. Investing in building the skills and capacity of staff as well as community by equipping them with tools.
    2. b. Developing more robust intake and referral systems.
      ​
  3. 3. Unmet needs
    ​ As our community grows it faces more problems; foster care, prison chaplaincy, opioids addictions, etc
    ​ We will pursue new areas of service in areas where there is the greatest need, least attention and most actionability.
    ​
  4. 4. Less resources
    ​ We are in a time of growing austerity.
    ​ We must be willing to…
    1. a. Stop doing things.
    2. b. Build political alliances across parties.
    3. c. Seek strategic partnerships with like minded organizations.
    4. d. Demonstrate excellence in fiscal transparency, accountability and impact.
    5. e. Be the best steward of zakat and sadqa.
    6. f. Amplify our voice.
    7. g. Focus on efforts that differentiate and distinguish us.



Our Aims…


Our Aims 2020–2022

1. Articulate & increase our impact .

2. Equip our team from entry level to the board.

3. Be sustainable in our interventions & operations.

1. Articulate &
​ increase our impact
​ —

i. Listen

Use data to inform our decisions & practices via…

  1. 1. Listening Campaigns
  2. 2. Long-Term Client Study
  3. 3. Discerning Root Causes

ii. Measure

Embrace metrics organization wide.

iii. Partner & Communicate

  1. 1. Take a partnerships first approach with community and the sector.
  2. 2. IFSSA is the hub for community needs.

iv. Strategic Program Growth

  1. 1. Build resilience through financial literacy and mentorship
  2. 2. Better meet basic needs: food security, affordable housing, vulnerable communities with unmet needs




2. Equip our team from entry level to the board
​ —


i. Equip & Invest in Team

We want our team (staff, board & volunteeers) to have the supports necessary to do their job in a fulfilling and excellent way, this means equipping them with role clarity, compassionate work environment, coaching, technical knowledge/training, practical tools and fostering creative problem solving.

ii. Locations

Have identified and secured locations that support better service delivery.

iii. Accreditation

  1. 1. Achieve Imagine Canada Certification - to bring governance, controls, and policy to a level of excellence
  2. 2. Identify and pursue standards that elevate internal excellence




3. Be sustainable in our interventions & operation
​ —


i. Diversified Funding & Strategic Reserve

  1. 1. Reduce dependency on individual government departments by prioritizing diversification
  2. 2. Build Reserves

ii. Operational Efficiency

  1. 1. Scale, Succession & Good Governance
  2. 2. Be the gold standard for transparency

iii. Responsible Consumption

  1. 1. Align purchasing with impact
