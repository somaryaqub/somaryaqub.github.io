Mission Question:
​ How can we
​ support the whole person
​ flourish in our community?


The whole person includes their mental, emotional, physical, familial, social, economic & spiritual well being.

Flourishing is in accordance with the person's expectations.

Our community includes the many geographical, cultural & faith networks that contribute to our identity.

We believe in a
​ Merciful & Generous Creator.

We begin with gratitude; gratitude for the work we do, who we do it with, and that people have trusted us with what’s difficult. ¶ We seek to imbue our work with mercy and generosity.¶ We work with an abundance of spirit – knowing that we do not have all the answers, that we must be frugal, responsible and transparent, that resources might be scarce, but that our imperative is to do what’s right and what’s right will follow.


We are Created to Serve.

IFSSA is both a means for people to find help and to live our fundamental purpose — to serve. We are being helped when we help. ¶ Serving others is spoken of within the Quran as medicine, and it speaks to the need within; an inner void that is filled through helping others.

“Created to Serve” is derived from Allah’s verse (Surah 3, Ayah 110), “You are the best people ever raised for the good of mankind because you have been raised to serve others.”


We are here to
​ grow (as) people.

We want to be the place that people do their best work. We see our work not as a series of interactions/transactions with clients but an investment in people, team & ourselves. In order to this work we must be developing ourselves.


We are emissaries
​ of a vast tradition.

We are the emissaries of a vast and rich tradition that informs and improves the way we care for people. For more than 30 years IFSSA has been supporting the community through complex challenges by acting on our values.


We are an integral part of the community we serve.

Community is vital to the work we do and who we are. ¶ We seek input, we serve as a platform for good initiatives, we are a starting point and a destination for those who serve. ¶ Because we want to go far we go together.





Who we serve…





Our Community…

IFSSA’s services are open to all, we serve all who come regardless of background while recognizing and addressing the specialized needs of the Muslim community.


Muslims are 5% of Alberta – 190,000 people. Muslims have been a part of Alberta since before Alberta was a province. Muslims are one of the youngest and fastest growing populations in Alberta. [Source: Statistic Canada 2016, analysis by ALIF Partners].


Our Clients…


IFSSA serves 4500 active clients, 1800 families every month — 1 out of 20 Muslims in Edmonton. 70% of IFSSA’s clients don’t use other services like Alberta Works or EMCN [Source: Edmonton’s Food Bank/ Edmonton Social Planning Council 2018 Study]. These are alarming numbers that necesitate us to try diferent approaches & better understand the needs of our clients.

Security, Safety & Growth

Currently, IFSSA works on addressing the security, safety and growth needs of people through a range of services including culturally appropriate food hampers, crisis support, domestic violence intervention, refugee support, preventative youth programming and by continuing to examine and address systemic challenges.

IFSSA’s clients face barriers when accessing services, these include…

  • Institutional distrust which has complex factors
  • Lack of awareness within systems of cultural conventions, norms, restrictions and preferences
  • Inability to access services due to language, stigma and unfamiliarity
  • Desire for a holistic care perspective that incorporates, mental, emotional, physical, social, & spiritual wellbeing

ii. Measure

“ No wind blows in favour of a ship without direction .”
​ —Michel de Montaigne.

Presently, we spend more time measuring outputs (ex. number of hampers distributed) rather than outcomes (ex. change in income, connectedness to community, etc). Identifying appropriate outcomes measures is complex, and time consuming but essential if we want to steer our work in the right direction, maximize positive impact, and properly steward the trust we’ve been given. .

To do this we plan to embrace Common Outcomes Reporting (COR) , an initiative of the City of Edmonton, United Way and Government of Alberta. COR contains a broad set of tools and methods to measure outcomes across a variety of program areas; volunteering, domestic violence, youth-at-risk, etc.

IFSSA is the first organization to embrace COR organzation wide . This work will compliment and enhance our research work, and may lead us to pursuing different ways of supporting our clients.

Objective 2: Metrics
​ a. IFSSA will put specific outcomes measures in place for each of its programs in 2020.
​
​ b. IFSSA will shift from measuring outputs to quantifying impact – from hampers distributed to people delivered from poverty, from hours spent to peoples increase in self-sufficiency.

iii. Partner

We will not go it alone. Whenever and wherever possible we will work to build solutions through partnerships. Providing holistic support that allows people to flourish will require an ecosystems approach.

Objective 3: Partnership's First
​ Existing and new programs will report on efforts to strengthen partnerships and articulate IFSSA's unique value in each segment we operate in for our annual report.

iii. Address Root Causes

Our clients come to us with a myriad of complex issues — what’s at the root of many of their problems is isolation and the need for information. We must begin to address these core issues. Three methods we will begin to explore are…

  1. 1. Mentorship
  2. 2. Collective kitchens
  3. 3. Financial literacy
Objective 4: Systemic Impact
​ IFSSA will support existing clients with more impactful programming using our existing resources by piloting new programs in early 2020.

iv. Guide Clients During Intake

Intake should be an opportunity to refer/enrol clients to services that will benefit them and their families. How well you get served is highly dependent how much the person serving you knows. Referrals can be highly complex given specific constraints client may face (language, mobility), the criteria of different programs (start dates, geography, income levels). Systems, process and technology are essential to improving this process.

IFSSA will work with technology companies, partner agencies, academia and government to build an intake process (for the sector) that better identifies client assets/needs while leveraging the shift to mobile, push technology, and improvements in databases.

Objective 5: Impactful Intake
​ By 2021 IFSSA will have a robust & consistent intake process that streamlines…
​ a. The assessment of more elements of our clients’ health.
​
​ b. Promotion of relevant programs & services, including internal/partner programs, community services (eg discounted pharmacy).
​
​ c. Continuous communication that informs clients on programs/services/community events that may benefit them.
​
​ d. Provides government with greater efficiencies by enabling greater sector collaboration and reducing replication.

v. Expand Services

IFSSA will work in the area of greatest need, least attention, most actionability. Areas we will begin to explore/pursue include…

  1. 1. Expanding Access (via After-hours support)
  2. 2. Expanding Refugee Supports (Sponsorship+)
  3. 3. Affordable Housing
  4. 4. Chaplaincy (Prisons, Bereavement)
  5. 5. Foster & Kinship Care

IFSSA will continue to be a supporting platform for community members pursuing social service work through mentorship & resource sharing. As we enter new program areas we will make mistakes & learn from them.

Objective 6: Grow Intelligently
​ a. To expand to admirably & sustainably serve underserved populations whom we would be the best advocates of.
​ b. To strategically chart out potential program areas for the future & identify the resources necessary to act.

vi. Celebrate Stories

FSSA’s ability to serve is tied directly to our ability to share engaging stories. These stories are not only important for donations but are vital part of inspiring clients and breaking negative cycles.

In a crowded media environment we will not be able to out spend larger non-profits that are attempting to engage the same audience. However, we can differentiate ourselves through compelling local stories and trusting that our work can speak for itself.

Objective 7: Celebrate Stories
​ To have a clear plan for communicating a positive, high frequency, non-annoying message in peoples lives that is charming, distinct & compels service.

vii. Engage Community

IFSSA has and must continue to be a platform for philanthropic mission-aligned community endeavours.

Objective 8: Engage Community
​ a. To have numerous meaningful ways for people to serve (2–3 types of volunteer offerings weekly).
​
​ b. For volunteering to be an investment and not a utility (volunteering is not a way to fill boxes, but a way to build people). We will demonstrate this by creating training/ personal development plans for volunteers by 2021.
​
​ c. To be a platform for new & emerging community driven issues. We will support community based social service initiatives.
​
​ d. Engage Muslim scholarship through the establishment of a local scholars council that supports frontline workers through knowledge and equips scholars/ community care providers with resources.
​
​ e. Strengthen our relationship with mosque & community organizations through the development of formal MOUs with mosques.

viii. Engage Government

IFSSA’s work depends on a strong productive relationship with governments at all levels and stripes. “There are no permanent allies or permanent enemies, only permanent interests.”

Objective 9: Engage Government
​ IFSSA will provide all political parties and government departments with policy aligned solutions that increase awareness of IFSSA’s services and its clients’ issues through direct engagement

ix. Define Enduring Values


Articulating IFSSA’s organizational values is crucial to the development of a high performance culture. IFSSA will work collectively and constructively with storytellers, poets and artists to meaningfully uncover our values.

Objective 10: Define Culture

ii. Building a Rahma (Mercy) Informed Workplace

As we work on pursuing trauma informed care for our clients we must also pursue being a trauma informed workplace; this requires developing excellent emotional & professional supports that take into consideration circumstance, equity and scale.

Objective 12: Rahma Informed Workplace
​ a. Develop managers to have more of the skills they need to support teams.
​
​ b. Increase management accountability through regular organizational wide check-ins that track satisfaction.
​
​ c. Create processes and systems that enable greater fluidity of staff to better distribute the workload, reduce stress and provide greater clarity.

iii. Competitively Compensate

IFSSA wants to retain and attract the best staff and be a community leader in fair compensation.

Objective 13: Competitive Compensation
​ a. Move towards alignment with AASIA recommended salary guidelines for the sector.
​
​ b. Increase IFSSA values aligned benefit programs for employees; this may include increasing the value of health care savings accounts, and supporting halal RRSP plans, mental health supports, etc.

iv. Make/Secure Programming/Office Space

All of our spaces are near end of lease or at end-of-life and it is vital that we find spaces that support staff to deliver services.

Objective 14: Location Planning
