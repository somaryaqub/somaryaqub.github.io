The opportunity

Al Rashid already serves thousands. The gaps between programs are where people fall.

The Al Rashid Foundation runs one of Canada's most comprehensive Muslim community ecosystems — the mosque, Edmonton Islamic Academy, Al Shatibi Institute, the “We Care” Unit, youth programs, seniors services, and more. But even the best-resourced institutions face the same challenge: the formal space between programs is where vulnerable community members go unmet.

Mental health stigma & access gaps

Even with Imam-led counselling, the demand for Islamically integrated, professionally supervised mental health support far exceeds what volunteer and religious staff can carry alone.

A community of 62 cultures

Edmonton's Muslim community is extraordinarily diverse. Navigating complex needs across languages, cultures, and immigration statuses requires trained, multilingual professionals.

Zakat distributed at scale

Large-scale zakat distribution without professional casework assessment risks missing deeper needs — and misses opportunities to enrol recipients in longer-term financial stability programs.

Newcomers and “We Care” Unit overlap

The “We Care” Unit supports newcomers and vulnerable Edmontonians — a dedicated social services navigator embedded at the mosque directly strengthens and extends this work.

The Al Rashid Foundation has built something rare: a full constellation of institutions — mosque, school, early learning, Quran institute, youth programs, seniors services — governed under one roof. A partnership with IslamicFamily adds a professional frontline care layer to this ecosystem that no single institution within it is positioned to provide alone.

How it works

Simple to start. Built to last.

We work with Al Rashid Foundation leadership to find the right fit across your ecosystem, then formalize the partnership with a clear, mutual agreement.

1

Ecosystem needs assessment

We meet with ARFC leadership — mosque, “We Care” Unit, EIA, and youth programs — to understand community priorities across your institutions and recommend the right tier and integration points.

2

MOU & service agreement

A clear Memorandum of Understanding sets out responsibilities, confidentiality standards, cost sharing, and reporting expectations — consistent with Al Rashid Foundation governance requirements.

3

Leadership orientation

Imams, EIA administrators, Al Shatibi staff, and key volunteers complete practical training on privacy, confidentiality, and referral pathways.

4

Embedding begins

Our outreach worker becomes a familiar presence — available during agreed hours, integrated with the mosque's rhythm, and coordinated with the “We Care” Unit and other Foundation programs.

5

Regular reporting & review

Quarterly reporting on activities and outcomes, structured for Al Rashid Foundation governance. Services adapt as trust deepens and community needs evolve.
