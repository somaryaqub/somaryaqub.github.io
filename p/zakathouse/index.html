A partnership built over three decades

MAC & IslamicFamily have a shared history of serving the Muslim community

We have worked together for over 30 years. Our relationship is not transactional - it is foundational. Across youth programming, family supports, inner-city services, job training, and emergency assistance, we have built trust together in the communities we both serve. This proposal builds naturally on that foundation. Directing zakat toward structured housing supports - through an established, trusted partner - is the next step in an already proven relationship.

The case for housing as foundation

Stable housing is not the destination - it is the starting line

Zakat is a dignified institution for poverty alleviation and social justice. Ideally, zakat is meant to be given to lift people out of poverty, not create dependencies.

- Effective Zakat in Canada, IslamicFamily (share.islamicfamily.ca/effectivezakat)

When a Muslim family cannot afford rent, every other intervention fails. Employment programs cannot work without a stable address. Financial literacy cannot take root when the eviction notice is coming. Children cannot learn when their home is at risk.

A one-time payment provides relief. A structured rent supplement - paired with the full ecosystem of social services - creates the conditions for a family to become fully self-sufficient and to no longer need zakat at all. That is the goal: not dependency, but dignity and sufficiency. We give so that the need to give eventually ends.

The program model

Housing as the hub - a full ecosystem of support

This is not a linear program. Housing stability is the foundation that makes every other support possible. Once a family is housed, our existing service infrastructure activates around them - drawing on 35 years of established programs, eligibility frameworks, and community relationships. The goal is movement: from subsidized housing to market housing, from crisis to capacity.

Stable housing - the foundation

Rent supplement paid directly to verified landlords. Housing secured first; everything else builds from here.

Financial literacy

Budgeting, savings, debt management, and banking - building the tools for independence.

Employment supports

Job readiness, credential recognition, résumé and referral - pathways to earned income.

Government benefit navigation

Connecting families to income supports, subsidies, and programs they are entitled to.

Mental health and wellbeing

Trauma-informed counselling and referral for individuals and families under stress.

Systems navigation

Legal aid referral, school enrollment, healthcare access, newcomer settlement - meeting families where they are.

Market housing transition

Affordable housing waitlists, savings plans, and tapered supplement as incomes grow.

None of these supports are new - they are existing IslamicFamily programs. Zakat funds the housing foundation. The service ecosystem is already in place.

Zakat compliance

Building on existing scholarly engagement

IslamicFamily's work on effective zakat distribution is grounded in Islamic jurisprudence and developed in collaboration with scholars. This program extends that body of work. Housing supplements for households in the fuqara and masakin categories are a well-established application of zakat - and a structured, time-limited program with a defined exit strategy honours both the obligation and the intent.

Zakat recipients have control of disbursements within a CRA and zakat compliant framework; audits ensure traceability and eliminate ambiguity about use of funds.

The program will operate within a Shariah compliance framework aligned with national standards being co-developed through the Canadian Council of Imams, with documentation available to zakat payers.

Scale and geography

Edmonton as proof of concept - national by design

Edmonton is the anchor site. IslamicFamily's 35-year presence, active housing development, and established assessment infrastructure make it the right place to prove the model. A six-province national coalition of Muslim social service organizations is already assembled. The program is built from year one to be replicated - with documentation, frameworks, and reporting tools designed for transfer to coalition partners.

Edmonton demonstrates. The coalition scales.

What we are asking for

Four commitments to build this together

This is a partnership, not a grant application. We are asking MAC Zakat House to work alongside IslamicFamily to build something durable - for Edmonton, and for the national Muslim community.

1

Zakat allocation commitment for 2026

A confirmed, directed zakat allocation toward the rent supplement program. A commitment - even phased and tied to reporting milestones - transforms our planning capacity and enables us to begin intake, coordinate partners, and serve families.

2

Co-development of a zakat community-based rent policy

Working together with the Canadian Council of Imams and partner organizations to establish shared eligibility standards, disbursement protocols, and reporting frameworks - creating a replicable national model grounded in fiqh and Canadian context.

3

Shared advocacy at all levels of government

Joining IslamicFamily in making the case to municipal, provincial, and federal governments for policies and funding that support halal-compliant affordable housing and recognize the role of faith-based organizations in housing solutions.

4

Co-development of a Halal Housing Summit

Convening Muslim housing advocates, scholars, social service organizations, and government partners from across Canada to align on a national vision, share models, and accelerate action on halal affordable housing.
