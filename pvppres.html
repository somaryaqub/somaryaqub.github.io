05 — Strategies

You don't have to
write to speak.

Original content is one path. But there's a whole menu — and some of the most impactful voices are curators, amplifiers, and learners in good standing.

★ High Impact
Invest, Investigate & Repost
Follow organizations and people close to issues important to those around you. Read deeply. Then amplify the best of what you find. You don't need the original thought — you need good judgment.
Strategy
Ask Questions
Post a genuine question rather than a take. Invites dialogue, signals curiosity, and rarely backfires. "What are the best resources on X?" builds community.
Strategy
Signal Solidarity
A like, a reshare, a comment of support. Sometimes the most important thing is showing up for someone else's voice rather than centering your own.
Strategy
Groundhog Day
You don't have to be on all the time. Get activated by a moment — a season, a news cycle, an anniversary — create a focused burst of effort, then go quiet. Intensity beats constant noise.
Strategy
Original Takes
When you have something uniquely yours to say — from lived experience, expertise, or your specific vantage point — say it. Don't waste that position.
06 — Watch Out

Be suspicious
of these.

Not every active voice is an effective one. Some patterns feel productive but aren't moving anything.

⚠
Preaching to the Choir
If your followers already agree with everything you say, you're not persuading anyone — you're performing. The goal is to move people, not just please them.
⚠
Catering to the Algorithm
Optimizing for engagement metrics — likes, reach, shares — often rewards heat over light. Viral isn't the same as impactful. Don't let the platform decide what you say.
⚠
Cultivating Someone Else's Audience
If the people following you aren't the people you actually need to reach, you're building in the wrong direction. Your audience is defined by your plan — not by who shows up.

  • Congenial ↔ Confrontational

  • 5–10 specific topics I want to write about
  • 3 voices I admire and why
  • What can I say that only I can say?

11 — Content in the AI Age

When everything looks
credible, nothing is.

Polished, professional, highly engaging AI content is now abundant. The challenge isn't access to information — it's the capacity to evaluate it.

01
Slow down before you share.
Polished ≠ true. Engaging ≠ accurate. The most dangerous content doesn't look wrong — it looks exactly like the things you trust. Pause before you amplify.
02
Return to people, not feeds.
The antidote to AI content abundance isn't more content — it's human connection. Genuine care, direct relationships, being present with the people you're actually trying to serve. Algorithms can't replicate that.
03
Cultivate spaces for contemplation.
Not every moment needs a take. Your plan should include silence — time to read slowly, sit with complexity, and let understanding develop before your voice does.

A voice plan in the AI age intensifies the need for intentionality, genuine connection, and discernment.
