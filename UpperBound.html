The Pitch

Social services isn't AI's last frontier.
It's its most important one.

While AI has transformed finance, healthcare, and logistics, frontline social workers are still spending 80% of their time on paperwork instead of people. That's not a resource problem. It's a design problem — and AI is about to make it catastrophically worse, or radically better.

The practical applications are immediate: automated referrals, multilingual omni-channel communication, forms that complete themselves, reporting that funders actually trust. But additive AI won't get us there. What's needed is a wholesale reimagining — one that puts data justice, UX, and game design at the foundation before a single model is trained.

This session makes the case, shows the live product, and hands the audience a framework they can use the next day.

The Framework

9 questions before every intake question

The People-First Intake Charter gives practitioners a decision tool — not just a values statement. Every data point must pass nine filters before it enters a case file.

01
Purpose
Who is this question actually for?
02
Insight
What does this teach us — and them?
03
Ownership
Can people move, edit, or delete this?
04
Timing
Could this wait until trust is built?
05
Experience
Cold form or human conversation?
06
Cost
What does this take from the relationship?
07
Risk
What if this were overheard? Breached?
08
Redundancy
Do we already know this another way?
09
Destination
Attached to a program — or a person?
