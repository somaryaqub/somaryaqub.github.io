Key Observations & Next Steps

50 units is achievable from Tier 1 alone Even at the conservative end, the 13 Tier 1 sites can yield ~82 units without touching the RM tier. The 4 RSM h12 sites carry disproportionate weight — the Highlands site alone could yield 14–20 units.
Paired opportunity — Sherwood (156 St) 9421 (RS) and 9429 (RSM h12) are immediately adjacent on 156 St NW, totalling ~1,223 m². Combined, they straddle both zone types and could be developed as a single site under RSM rules.
Tier 3 standouts: 4 large RM lots The four lots over 2,000 m² (North Glenora ×2, Queen Alexandra, Spruce Ave) are genuinely standalone-feasible at apartment scale — 64–106 units each. The Spruce Ave site at $593,500 assessed is particularly notable given its size.
RS unit cap under review As of June 2025, Edmonton City Council was debating reducing the 8-unit cap on mid-block RS lots. Confirm the current bylaw status before any development permit application on RS sites.
Lot dimensions still needed This analysis uses lot area only. Actual width & depth (from yourtruhome.com/edmonton-land-size or a land title search) needed to confirm minimum 30m depth (RS) and buildable envelope. Corner vs. mid-block status affects unit caps.
Unit estimates are ballpark only All unit counts assume ~75 m² avg unit size and do not account for parking requirements, amenity area, alley access, or site-specific constraints. A development consultant review is required before any acquisition decision.
