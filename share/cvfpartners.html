Civic Youth Fellowship

A coalition of six Edmonton organizations working together to open the door to municipal leadership for youth from equity-deserving communities.

About the Fellowship City of Edmonton Program Page
The Coalition

Six organizations, one shared commitment

Each partner brings a different community, a different history, and the same belief: that municipal government should reflect the people it serves.

The Africa Centre

The largest pan-African organization in Western Canada, building cultural awareness, competency, and community strength across Alberta.

Visit africacentre.ca

ASSIST Community Services Centre

Settlement services, language instruction, and family programming that welcome newcomers into Edmonton with dignity and inclusion.

Visit assistcsc.org

Canadian Race Relations Foundation

A federal crown corporation raising public awareness of racism and funding community work that strengthens equity across Canada.

Visit crrf-fcrr.ca

kihêw waciston

MacEwan University's Indigenous Centre — "eagle's nest" in Cree — a home for ceremony, culture, and academic support for Indigenous students.

Visit kihêw waciston
Shown with MacEwan University's institutional logo

IslamicFamily

Serving Edmonton's Muslim and newcomer communities for more than 35 years through compassion, advocacy, and essential services. Initiator of the Fellowship.

Visit islamicfamily.ca

The City of Edmonton

Home to more than a million people on Treaty 6 territory, and host to the Fellowship placements inside Council and City Hall.

Visit edmonton.ca

We acknowledge that the Civic Youth Fellowship operates on Amiskwaciwâskahikan, within Treaty 6 territory — the traditional gathering place for many Indigenous peoples. We honour the history, languages, and cultures of the First Nations, Métis, and Inuit who call this territory home.
