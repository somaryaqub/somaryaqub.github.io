Considerations and risks by option

Option 1 — resident-first

  • No financial return to owner — leaves operating budget unsupported
  • Cleanest mission alignment: residents pay only what metering actually costs
  • Right choice only if other operating revenue sources exist

Option 2 — recommended

  • Residents pay $20/month above CARMA's actual metering cost
  • Revenue must demonstrably flow back to residents (lower rents, better services) to satisfy mission framing
  • CMHC/AHC may scrutinize ongoing fee extraction from affordable housing residents
  • Zero upfront construction capital

Option 3 — balanced (not optimal for either goal)

  • $96K upfront is modest against a $30M+ project
  • Ongoing income roughly half of Option 2 over the full term
  • CARMA nets significantly more per suite than in Options 1 or 2

Option 4 — solves the wrong problem

  • Addresses construction capital, which is not the constraint
  • Residents pay $20/month premium with zero ongoing benefit returning to the project
  • CARMA keeps approximately $428K of the fee premium over the 15-year term
