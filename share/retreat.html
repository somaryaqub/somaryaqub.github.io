9:20 – 10:50 90 min

Sharing pre-work frameworks

Each manager shares what they brought. We listen to understand before we connect — this is not a debate, it's a disclosure.

When it's your turn, share these four things: your north star sentence · your most important goal · your biggest revenue opportunity · the conversation you most need to have today.

Your job in this block

  • Share your four things clearly in 10–12 minutes. Be specific — numbers, names, timelines where you have them.

  • Notice what surprises you, what connects to your own work, and what you didn't know about other areas.

12:50 – 2:20 90 min

Values exercise

We refresh the language of who IslamicFamily is — drawn from what we've actually lived this year, not what we aspire to say about ourselves. The goal is language that feels earned.

Your job in this block

  • Write specific examples — a moment, a decision, a story. "We are compassionate" is a claim; a story is evidence.
  • Push for language that could only describe IslamicFamily. If it could be any nonprofit, it's not ours.
  • Name the honest gap. Where did we fall short of our own values this year? That conversation is what makes the draft real.
  • Stay open to words that surprise you. The unexpected ones often carry the most life.

3:05 – 3:30 25 min

Budget & targets alignment

A fast pass — not a budget meeting. We surface mismatches between our priorities and our spending, name our revenue commitments, and build the list of things that need to go to the board.

Your job in this block

  • Name one place where your current budget doesn't match what you said matters today. Be honest — this isn't an audit, it's a planning conversation.
  • State your revenue commitment specifically: not "explore grants" but what you would actually do, by when, and what you need to move it.
  • Flag anything that needs board attention before the year begins.

Commitments & close
