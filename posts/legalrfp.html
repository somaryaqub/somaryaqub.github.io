Project Context

A legally complex, mission-driven development.

IslamicFamily is developing 96 halal affordable housing units in north Edmonton — four four-storey buildings with 264 bedrooms, designed for larger and multigenerational families. The project is developed by IslamicFamily (Islamic Family & Social Services Association) in partnership with a design-build general contractor under a lump-sum design-build delivery model.

Financing is deliberately diverse. The project draws on co-applied federal and provincial grants, City of Edmonton land disposition support, and the Halal Affordable Housing Investment Fund — Canada's first institutional-quality, Shariah-compliant housing investment vehicle, developed in partnership with SVX and structured with legal counsel from Pallett Valo LLP.

Why this engagement is specialized: The purchase agreement must satisfy the requirements of multiple funders simultaneously — each with distinct reporting obligations, use-of-funds restrictions, and compliance triggers — while being fully Shariah-compliant and workable within Alberta's real estate and nonprofit regulatory frameworks. This is not a standard residential or commercial purchase agreement.

The successful lawyer will work closely with IslamicFamily's Executive Director and will need to coordinate with the existing Islamic finance legal team (Pallett Valo LLP) and the Owner Representative being engaged concurrently. Familiarity with the existing fund structure documentation is expected before engagement begins.

Scope of Work

What you'll be engaged to do.

The primary deliverable is a comprehensive purchase agreement for the subject property in north Edmonton. Beyond drafting, the engagement encompasses legal advice on related structures and risks. The scope is organized into six interconnected areas:

1. Purchase Agreement — Core Structure

  • Conditions precedent tied to multi-source funding milestones
  • Closing mechanics that accommodate staggered fund closings
  • Clear title transfer provisions with Islamic finance lien/charge structure
  • Representations and warranties appropriate to a nonprofit purchaser
  • Risk allocation across IslamicFamily, the contractor, and funders

2. Multi-Source Funding Integration

  • Grant co-application compliance: articulate funder priority, reporting obligations, and spending restrictions within the agreement
  • Halal investment fund provisions: ensure agreement structure does not conflict with musharakah/ijara or other Islamic finance instruments
  • Subordination and intercreditor logic between government funders and private Islamic investors
  • Mechanisms for future funding tranches and amendments

3. Shariah Compliance

  • Review and confirm the agreement is free of riba (interest), gharar (excessive uncertainty), and maysir (speculative elements)
  • Structure payment and security mechanisms in accordance with Shariah principles, in coordination with Pallett Valo LLP
  • Advise on any standard commercial clauses that may require modification for compliance
  • Document Shariah compliance rationale for investor and board reporting

4. Change Order Process

  • Define a clear, enforceable change order protocol within the design-build lump-sum contract context
  • Establish thresholds: what triggers a change order vs. falls within contractor scope
  • IslamicFamily approval authorities and timelines
  • Funder notification obligations when change orders affect funded scope
  • Documentation standards and dispute escalation path

5. Deliverables, Quality & Durability Standards

  • Define final deliverables with specificity: unit specifications, common areas, accessibility features, BUILT GREEN Platinum standards
  • Building performance standards and durability benchmarks tied to affordable housing life-cycle expectations
  • Deficiency rectification process and holdback provisions
  • Warranty terms: new home warranty, structural, mechanical, envelope
  • Substantial completion and final acceptance criteria

6. Default, Remedy & Dispute Resolution

  • Contractor default provisions appropriate to a nonprofit owner with grant obligations
  • Termination rights and notice requirements
  • Funder notification obligations on material breach or default
  • Dispute resolution mechanism: preference for negotiation / mediation before arbitration
  • Governing law: Province of Alberta

Shariah Compliance

Why this isn't an add-on — it's foundational.

The Halal Affordable Housing Investment Fund is structured as Canada's first institutional-quality Shariah-compliant housing investment vehicle. Its investors — individual Muslims and impact investors who have specifically sought a halal alternative — have made this commitment on the basis of Shariah compliance. Any purchase agreement that introduces riba, gharar, or other prohibited elements would undermine the integrity of the fund and IslamicFamily's obligations to its investors and community.

The agreement must be something that any Muslim investor in the fund can review and confirm: this is how we said we would do it. Compliance is not a legal formality — it is a matter of institutional integrity.

The successful lawyer must be willing to work in close coordination with Pallett Valo LLP, which has provided the existing fund legal structure. They do not need to be Islamic finance specialists themselves, but they must have demonstrated willingness to learn, adapt standard instruments, and take Shariah review seriously as a binding constraint — not an afterthought. Where any standard clause requires modification, we expect proactive identification and proposed alternatives.

Coordination requirement: The selected lawyer must engage directly with Pallett Valo LLP at the outset to review the existing fund structure documentation and ensure the purchase agreement is structurally coherent with the Islamic finance instruments already in place. This coordination is not optional.
