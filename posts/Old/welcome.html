For years I've kept notes in the margins of grant applications, board memos, and late-night strategy docs. Observations about how systems work — and who they work for. Questions about whether the way we've organized Muslim social services in Canada is actually serving our communities, or just serving the bureaucracies that fund us.

This is where those notes become something more.

I'm not sure what this will be exactly. A newsletter feels too transactional. A blog feels too 2009. I think of it more as a thinking space — somewhere I can work through the ideas that keep showing up across my work at IslamicFamily, at Flourishing Systems, in the classroom at the University of Alberta.

A few threads I expect to pull on:

  • Data justice and what we owe to the people we serve
  • The infrastructure problem in Muslim charitable work
  • What "community-centered" actually has to look like operationally
  • Governance — the stuff no one likes to talk about until it's on fire

If any of that lands, I'd be glad to have you reading along.
