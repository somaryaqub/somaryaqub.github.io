The Project

A different kind of housing development.

we are working on a halal affordable housing project in Edmonton — 264 bedrooms, 92% of which have 2 or more bedrooms, and 30% designed with 4–5 bedrooms for extended and multigenerational families. Construction is underway.

The project is developed by IslamicFamily in partnership with a design-build contractor, with financing through the Halal Affordable Housing Investment Fund — Canada's first institutional-quality, Shariah-compliant housing investment vehicle, developed with SVX and legal counsel from Pallett Valo LLP.

Though Muslims represent less than 5% of Canada's population, we are more than 11% of those living in affordable housing. The average family we serve has 7 members, with typical wait times for adequate housing running 3–4 years.

This project speaks to something broader. The values embedded extended family living, multigenerational design, community accountability, and ethical finance — are shared by Indigenous peoples and many other communities consistently underserved by conventional housing models.

The Role

The Owner Representative's mandate.

In a design-build model, the architects and engineers report to the builder — not to us. The Owner Representative (OR) is our independent check on design quality, cost management, and schedule integrity. You answer squarely to IslamicFamily.

Why this role matters here: IslamicFamily is a mission-driven nonprofit, not a developer. We have extraordinary community knowledge and institutional depth — but we need a technical expert at our side to ensure that a $37M lump-sum contract is managed with the rigour that protects our community's investment.

The engagement begins now, during the remaining design and procurement phase, and intensifies through construction. We are open to proposals from both senior individual project managers and established firms.

Scope of Work

What you'll be responsible for.

Cost Management

Monitor budget against the lump-sum contract. Flag deviations early. Advise on change orders and contingency deployment.

Design & Quality Review

Review design submittals, engineering drawings, and RFIs. Represent IslamicFamily's interests in design decisions that affect quality, function, or community suitability.

Schedule Oversight

Track construction milestones and identify schedule risks early. Coordinate with builder while maintaining independent accountability to IslamicFamily.

Procurement & Procurement QA

Advise on major procurement decisions. Ensure contractor compliance with specifications and contracted scope.

Safety & Site Oversight

Monitor construction site safety compliance. Escalate any concerns to IslamicFamily leadership promptly.

Document Control

Maintain a clear, organized record of submittals, approvals, RFIs, meeting minutes, and change orders throughout the engagement.

About IslamicFamily

More than three decades of community trust.

IslamicFamily (Islamic Family & Social Services Association) is an Edmonton-based nonprofit serving equity-seeking communities across Alberta. We provide a wide range of social services, housing support, and financial empowerment programming — rooted in community, accountable to those we serve, and unapologetically values-driven.

Our work has been recognized through Imagine Canada accreditation, the Government of Alberta Inspiration Award, the Canadian Mental Health Association's Professional Service Award, a Justice Innovation Award, and both the Canadian Institute of Planners and Alberta Professional Planners Institute Award for affordable housing research.

Thi is the culmination of a decade of housing research, partnership-building, and removing structural barriers that have frustrated previous attempts. It represents our commitment to becoming a permanent community infrastructure provider — not just a project-based charity.

More information: Full project details, fund structure, and community impact data available at halalfund.ca. For questions, contact Omar Yaqub at omary@islamicfamily.ca.
